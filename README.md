## Abstract

Quantum machine learning (QML) provides parameter-efficient substitutes for traditional deep networks in medical imaging settings with constrained computational resources. However, deployment on noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) devices is difficult due to manual architecture design and decoherence constraints, particularly when circuit depth is limited. We introduce **D-HAQNAS** ‚ÄĒ a differentiable hardware-aware framework that uses end-to-end differentiable optimization to find energy-efficient quantum topologies.

Our method outperforms evolutionary methods in architecture search by up to **15√ó**. Three essential elements are integrated: **(1)** a differentiable super-circuit with sigmoid-gated architectural parameters enabling gradient-based single-qubit gate topology search; **(2)** hardware-aware penalty terms that constrain gate count and circuit depth *during* training; and **(3)** weighted residual fusion to stabilize hybrid classical‚Äďquantum gradient flow.

D-HAQNAS achieves **96.21% accuracy** on PneumoniaMNIST ‚ÄĒ statistically comparable to classical baselines (p = 0.279) but with **78.6% fewer trainable parameters** and a **31.5% decrease in gate count** (54 ‚Üí 37). On the severely data-limited RetinaMNIST benchmark (1,080 samples, 5-class diabetic retinopathy classification), D-HAQNAS demonstrates a statistically significant **+4.17 percentage point** improvement over classical models (57.50% vs. 53.33%, paired t-test p = 0.031), establishing parameter-efficient hybrid quantum-classical networks as a viable strategy for clinical settings constrained by data scarcity.

**Index Terms** ‚ÄĒ quantum machine learning, neural architecture search, NISQ devices, medical imaging, hybrid quantum-classical networks

## Motivation

Modern deep learning has transformed medical imaging ‚ÄĒ but deployment constraints remain severe:

| Challenge | Reality |
|---|---|
| **Parameter count** | ResNet-18 requires 11.7M parameters |
| **Inference latency** | Unsuitable for point-of-care diagnostics |
| **Data scarcity** | Clinical datasets routinely contain <5,000 samples |
| **NISQ constraints** | Circuit depth and gate count limits ignored by most QML research |
| **Architecture design** | Manual circuit design doesn't scale; evolutionary NAS takes 8+ hours |

QML promises parameter efficiency through quantum superposition and entanglement. However, existing approaches evaluate manually-constructed circuits under idealized, noise-free simulations ‚ÄĒ creating a fundamental gap between published results and NISQ hardware viability.

**D-HAQNAS closes this gap** by making hardware constraints a *training objective*, not a post-hoc filter.

### Differentiable Super-Circuit

The quantum branch implements a hardware-efficient variational ansatz with **L = 3 layers** over **n_q ‚ąą {4, 6} qubits**:

**Angle Embedding (per qubit i):**
```
RY(ŌÜŠĶĘ),  ŌÜŠĶĘ = arctan(fŠĶĘ) ‚ąą (‚ąíŌÄ/2, ŌÄ/2)
```
arctan preprocessing guarantees bounded, stable rotation angles independent of feature magnitude ‚ÄĒ unlike amplitude embedding which requires 2‚ĀŅŠĶ† features with normalization constraints.

**Parameterized Rotation Block (per qubit i, layer l):**
```
U_rot^(l,i) = ŌÉ(őĪ_{l,i,2}) RZ(őł_{l,i,2}) ¬∑ ŌÉ(őĪ_{l,i,1}) RY(őł_{l,i,1}) ¬∑ ŌÉ(őĪ_{l,i,0}) RZ(őł_{l,i,0})
```
where `ŌÉ(őĪ) = (1 + e‚ĀĽŠĶÖ)‚ĀĽ¬Ļ` is the sigmoid gate activation probability. Gates with `ŌÉ(őĪ) ‚Č• 0.5` remain active; those below are pruned to identity. The RZ-RY-RZ sequence spans the full SU(2) group.

**Entangling Layer (fixed, not searchable):**
```
U_CNOT^(l) = ‚ąŹ CNOT(i, (i+1) mod n_q)
```
Ring CNOT topology is compatible with Google Sycamore and IBM heavy-hex hardware. Fixing entanglement maintains a tractable search space.

**Search Space:** For n_q = 6, L = 3: **54 differentiable single-qubit gates** + 18 fixed CNOT gates = 72 total decisions.

**Discrete Circuit Extraction:**
```
gate_{l,i,j} = active   if ŌÉ(őĪ_{l,i,j}) ‚Č• 0.5
               pruned    if ŌÉ(őĪ_{l,i,j}) < 0.5
```

**Gradient Computation** via parameter-shift rule:
```
‚ąā/‚ąāőł_{l,i,j} ‚ü®Šļź‚āĖ‚ü© = ¬Ĺ [‚ü®Šļź‚āĖ‚ü©_{őł+ŌÄ/2} ‚ąí ‚ü®Šļź‚āĖ‚ü©_{őł‚ąíŌÄ/2}]
```
Complexity: **O(3Ln_q ¬∑ B)** per batch, requiring two circuit evaluations per parameter.

|---|---|
| ResNet-18 Layer 4 (fine-tuned) | 1.5M |
| Quantum circuit (őĪ + őł) | ~160 |
| Fusion layer (W_q, ő≤) | ~3,300 |
| Classifier head | ~10 |
| **Total Trainable** | **2.5M** |
| Classical ResNet-18 (full) | 11.7M |
| **Reduction** | **78.6%** |

## Results

### PneumoniaMNIST (Binary Classification)

**Dataset:** 4,708 training / 624 test samples (28√ó28 grayscale chest X-rays, balanced binary classes). Evaluated via 5-fold stratified cross-validation.

| Model | Accuracy (%) | AUC-ROC | Total Params | Trainable Params |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Classical ResNet-18 | 95.68 ¬Ī 0.41 | 0.989 | 11.7M | 11.7M |
| MLP Ablation | 96.69 ¬Ī 0.27 | 0.991 | 11.7M | 11.7M |
| **D-HAQNAS** | **96.21 ¬Ī 0.57** | **0.992** | **11.2M** | **2.5M** |

> **Paired t-test vs. Classical:** p = 0.279 (not significant ‚ÄĒ competitive parity)
> **Paired t-test vs. MLP:** p = 0.043 (MLP ablation edges D-HAQNAS on this well-powered dataset)

**Key takeaway:** D-HAQNAS matches classical performance in AUC (0.992) with 78.6% fewer trainable parameters. On large, balanced datasets, the primary gain is *parameter efficiency*, not raw accuracy. The MLP ablation remains competitive ‚ÄĒ this is expected, and the paper is transparent about it.

### RetinaMNIST (5-Class Diabetic Retinopathy)

**Dataset:** 1,080 training / 400 test samples (28√ó28 grayscale fundus images, ~216 samples per class). The most challenging MedMNIST benchmark due to extreme data scarcity and fine-grained class imbalance. Evaluated via 5-fold stratified cross-validation.

| Model | Accuracy (%) | AUC | Trainable Params |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classical ResNet-18 | 53.33 ¬Ī 2.14 | 0.721 | 11.7M |
| **D-HAQNAS** | **57.50 ¬Ī 1.89** | **0.748** | **2.5M** |

> **Absolute gain:** +4.17 percentage points
> **Relative improvement:** +7.8%
> **Paired t-test:** p = 0.031 ‚úď (statistically significant at őĪ = 0.05)

**Key takeaway:** Under severe data scarcity (1,080 samples, 5 classes), D-HAQNAS provides a *statistically significant* accuracy and AUC improvement. We attribute this primarily to the regularizing effect of parameter-efficient quantum processing, which reduces overfitting when training data is scarce. Whether this reflects parameter regularization or an intrinsic quantum effect remains an open research question.

### Hardware Efficiency

Circuit analysis on PneumoniaMNIST (n_q = 6):

| Metric | Fixed Topology | D-HAQNAS | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total Possible Gates | 54 | 54 | ‚ÄĒ |
| Active Gates (post-search) | 54 | **37** | **‚ąí31.5%** |

| NISQ Compatibility Score | 78.4 / 100 | **86.0 / 100** | **+7.6 pts** |

The NISQ Compatibility Score is defined as `S = 100 √ó (1 ‚ąí ńď)`, where `ńď` is the weighted average gate error rate using IBM Quantum parameters (p‚āĀ = 0.1% single-qubit, p‚āā = 1.0% two-qubit).

**Pruning dynamics:** Gate density declines from 100% (epoch 0) to 68.5% (epoch 15), stabilizing by epoch 10 following őĽ warmup completion. Architecture search runs in **32 minutes total** ‚ÄĒ **15√ó faster** than evolutionary quantum NAS (8+ hours).

### Noise Robustness

Evaluated using PennyLane `default.mixed` density matrix simulator with IBM Quantum device parameters:

| Noise Channel | Parameter |
|---|---|
| Single-qubit depolarizing | p‚āĀ = 0.1% |
| Two-qubit depolarizing (CNOT) | p‚āā = 1.0% |
| Thermal relaxation (T‚āĀ) | 100 ¬Ķs (amplitude damping) |
| Thermal relaxation (T‚āā) | 80 ¬Ķs (phase damping) |
| Readout misclassification | 1% |

Models are trained on ideal (noiseless) simulation and evaluated across four noise profiles: ideal, depolarizing-only, thermal-only, and IBM-realistic (combined).

| Model | Ideal Acc. | IBM-Realistic Acc. | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classical ResNet-18 | 95.68% | 87.0% | 90.9% |
| **D-HAQNAS** | **96.21%** | **95.2%** | **98.9%** |

> Retention = (Noisy Accuracy / Ideal Accuracy) √ó 100%

**D-HAQNAS achieves 98.9% performance retention under full IBM-realistic noise** ‚ÄĒ 8 percentage points above the classical baseline. The mechanism is direct: 31.5% fewer active gates means fewer decoherence sources. Since CNOT error rates are 10‚Äď15√ó higher than single-qubit gates, the architecture search's learned sparsity disproportionately reduces error accumulation.

### Ablation Study

Evaluated on RetinaMNIST with a simplified backbone (no Layer 4 fine-tuning) to isolate component contributions:

| Variant | Accuracy (%) | Active Gates | őĒ Baseline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classical Only | 48.50 | 0 | ‚ÄĒ |
| Quantum Only | 53.25 | 31 | +4.75% |
| Hybrid (Concatenation) | 50.25 | 31 | +1.75% |
| Hybrid (Fixed Topology) | 49.75 | 45 | +1.25% |
| **D-HAQNAS (Full)** | **51.00** | **31** | **+2.50%** |

> Note: Quantum Only (53.25%) > D-HAQNAS Full (51.00%) in the simplified backbone regime due to the compression bottleneck: without Layer 4 fine-tuning, classical features are weaker, and the n_q-dimensional compression layer creates an information bottleneck that pure quantum processing avoids. This anomaly disappears in the full model (Table II: 57.50%), confirming that Layer 4 fine-tuning resolves the bottleneck. The lower ablation range (48.50‚Äď51.00%) vs. main results (53.33‚Äď57.50%) confirms backbone sensitivity.

Each component contributes measurably. Residual fusion adds +0.75% over concatenation; architecture search adds +1.25% with a 31% gate reduction.

    n_layers=3,
    n_classes=2,
    lambda_hw=0.02         # Hardware penalty coefficient (őĽ_max)
)

# Train with joint architecture + weight optimization
optimizer = torch.optim.AdamW(
    model.parameters(), lr=1e-4, weight_decay=1e-5
)

for epoch in range(15):
    for batch_x, batch_y in train_loader:
        output = model(batch_x)

        # L_total = L_CE(őł|őĪ) + őĽ¬∑L_hw(őĪ)
        # Hardware penalty warmed up linearly over first 5 epochs
        loss = model.compute_loss(output, batch_y, epoch=epoch)

        optimizer.zero_grad()
        loss.backward()
        optimizer.step()

# Extract discrete NISQ-deployable circuit
discrete_circuit = model.extract_discrete_circuit(threshold=0.5)
print(f"Active gates: {discrete_circuit.count_gates()}")  # ~37 for PneumoniaMNIST

## Limitations & Future Work

### Current Limitations

1. **Simulation scale:** Restricted to 4‚Äď6 qubits due to exponential memory scaling in statevector simulation.
2. **Dataset scale:** Validated on MedMNIST benchmarks (1,480‚Äď5,332 samples); benefits in data-scarce regimes may not transfer to clinical-scale datasets like ChestX-ray14 (112,120 images).
3. **Noise model:** Averaged IBM Quantum parameters; real hardware introduces qubit-specific calibration drift, crosstalk, and leakage beyond the averaged model.
4. **Search space scope:** Only single-qubit gate activations are optimized; CNOT entanglement topology is fixed.
5. **Task scope:** Binary and 5-class classification only; multi-label pathology detection is unvalidated.
6. **Quantum vs. classical attribution:** Whether RetinaMNIST gains reflect parameter regularization or an intrinsic quantum phenomenon remains an open question.

### Future Directions

- [ ] **Entanglement topology search** ‚ÄĒ extend NAS to CNOT connectivity, not just single-qubit activations
- [ ] **Hardware-in-the-loop** ‚ÄĒ run architecture search directly on IBM Quantum / Rigetti backends
- [ ] **Multi-objective Pareto optimization** ‚ÄĒ balance accuracy, circuit depth, and CNOT budget
- [ ] **Clinical-scale validation** ‚ÄĒ ChestX-ray14 (112K), MIMIC-CXR (377K)
- [ ] **Multi-label pathology classification** ‚ÄĒ extend beyond ordinal/binary tasks
- [ ] **3D medical imaging** ‚ÄĒ CT/MRI volumetric encoding schemes
- [ ] **Beyond 6 qubits** ‚ÄĒ modular quantum architectures, tensor-network inspired designs
- [ ] **Transfer learning** ‚ÄĒ pre-trained quantum circuits for few-shot diagnostics

### Theoretical Positioning

D-HAQNAS does not claim quantum computational advantage in the asymptotic complexity sense. The contribution is methodological: demonstrating that differentiable quantum architecture search can produce hardware-feasible circuits that perform competitively in resource-constrained regimes, exploring parameter efficiency under data scarcity, noise robustness through learned sparsity, and hardware-aware optimization as an integrated training objective rather than a post-hoc constraint.

## Broader Impact

**Potential benefits:** Lower computational requirements enable deployment in under-resourced hospitals and remote telemedicine units. Parameter-efficient models reduce the need for large labeled datasets, critical for rare disease diagnosis. Practical benchmarks on realistic noise models can guide near-term quantum hardware development.

**Responsible deployment considerations:** Results on MedMNIST benchmarks do not constitute regulatory approval. Medical datasets may reflect demographic biases requiring explicit fairness auditing. Quantum circuit decisions are non-trivial to explain to clinical practitioners. Quantum hardware remains expensive and geographically concentrated.

> **This is research-stage technology. Clinical deployment requires regulatory approval, extensive prospective validation, and interpretability analysis.**
